1. Purpose

This Enterprise Risk Management ("ERM") Policy establishes the framework within which Meridian Care Technologies, Inc. ("MCT") identifies, analyzes, treats, monitors, and reports on enterprise risks — including cybersecurity, financial, operational, regulatory, strategic, reputational, and clinical-product risks. It provides the integration layer ensuring that cybersecurity risk decisions made under POL-001 are visible in, and consistent with, MCT's broader enterprise risk posture.

2. Scope

This Policy applies to risks affecting MCT's ability to achieve its mission and strategic objectives. It governs all risks regardless of source — internal or external, intentional or accidental — and regardless of risk type. It is integrated with the Cybersecurity Risk Appetite Statement (RAS-2025-12), the Information Security Policy (POL-001), and the operational risk processes maintained by the CFO's office.

4. Risk Lifecycle

4.1 Identification. Risks may be identified through any of: continuous monitoring, control testing, internal audit, third-party assessments, threat intelligence, post-incident review, employee escalation, customer feedback, regulator inquiry, or strategic planning. Any employee may submit a risk observation via the GRC intake form.

4.2 Analysis. Identified risks are analyzed for likelihood, impact, velocity (how quickly impact would manifest), and detectability. Cybersecurity risks use the 5×5 likelihood/impact scale defined in PROC-RM-01 and produce inherent and residual scores. FAIR (Factor Analysis of Information Risk) is used to estimate annualized loss expectancy where the data supports a quantitative estimate.

4.3 Treatment. Each open risk has a defined treatment strategy: Mitigate (reduce likelihood and/or impact via controls), Avoid (eliminate the activity giving rise to the risk), Transfer (e.g., insurance, contractual indemnity), or Accept (knowingly bear). Risk acceptance is approved by the Audit & Risk Committee for residual scores above the Risk Appetite Statement thresholds; below appetite, acceptance may be approved by the relevant executive owner with notification to the GRC Manager.

4.4 Monitoring. Open risks are reviewed monthly by the Steering Committee for the relevant risk category, and quarterly by the Audit & Risk Committee. Treatment progress is tracked against committed milestones.

4.5 Closure. A risk is closed when its residual likelihood × impact falls to ≤2, or when the underlying activity is decommissioned, or upon successful completion of treatment milestones with sustained evidence of effectiveness across two consecutive review periods.

5. Integration with Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity is treated as a category of enterprise risk, not a separate discipline. The Cybersecurity Risk Register is a sub-ledger of the enterprise risk view. Material cybersecurity risks (residual score ≥10 or High severity) are surfaced into the enterprise risk view reviewed by the Audit & Risk Committee. The CISO and CFO meet monthly to reconcile cybersecurity risk register entries against the broader enterprise view.

Aggregate annualized cyber loss expectancy (ALE) is the primary quantitative bridge between the cybersecurity register and the enterprise view. As of Q1 2026, aggregate ALE across the open cyber risk register is approximately $1.62M, against a Board-approved appetite of $2M.
