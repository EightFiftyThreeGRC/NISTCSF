1. Purpose

This Playbook catalogs the production and in-development detection use cases operated against MCT log sources in Datadog Cloud SIEM. It implements the continuous monitoring requirements of POL-001 §3.11 and is the primary content artifact backing the Detect (DE) function of the NIST CSF profile.

This document is content-management oriented: it describes what MCT detects, not how (specific queries and thresholds are maintained as Datadog Sigma-format rules in the mct-detection-content repository). It is intended to be read alongside the SIEM Configuration Standard (STD-LOG-01) and the Alert Triage Procedure (PROC-AlertTriage).

5. Gap Analysis

The detection program is honest about the following gaps. They are reviewed quarterly at the Security Steering Committee and tracked in the cyber risk register.

5.1 Telemetry-driven gaps

Legacy ETL cluster (R4). Host-level visibility is partial; database-layer audit is manual. The two backlog use cases targeting this cluster cannot be fully implemented before decommission. Compensating controls: edge-firewall egress allowlist with logging; CrowdStrike Falcon on cluster hosts (use case EX-01 covers); manual quarterly review of database audit dumps.

Internal-only Aurora reader (P3). Query-level pgAudit is not enabled; lateral-movement and exfiltration detection on the reader is limited to connection-level events.

East-west service-mesh telemetry. mTLS traffic logs are not yet centralized pending zero-trust rollout (FY26 H1). LM-02 backlog will resolve as part of that work.

5.2 Tuning-driven gaps (R3)

High false-positive rate on tier-1 alerts (~28%, Q1 2026). Acknowledged debt. Driven primarily by IA-04 (synthetic monitoring traffic), DI-02 (analyst tooling), CA-04 (legacy build agent), EX-04 (Snowflake auto-scale), and PR-03 (CI/CD churn). The Q1 2026 tuning sprint (one engineer at 50% allocation) is targeting these five and is forecast to close R3 to a residual score of 5 by June 30, 2026.

Snowflake compute anomaly runbook (EX-04) is draft. Use case is in Tuning until the runbook is approved.

5.3 Coverage gaps not yet driven into a use case

Insider-threat / UEBA tuning on production access logs is captured separately as RISK R7.

Patient-portal CIAM (Auth0) anomaly detection is captured under RISK R8 and is in Auth0-native rules; integration into Datadog SIEM is on the FY26 backlog.

6. Lifecycle of a Detection Use Case

Idea — proposed by analyst, engineer, threat-intel feed, post-incident review, or red team.

Backlog — captured in this catalog with telemetry/dependency notes.

Drafting — Sigma rule authored in mct-detection-content; reviewed by D&R engineer.

Tuning — deployed in observation mode for at least 14 days; FP rate measured against threshold (target <10%); runbook drafted.

Production — promoted; bound to a triage tier; runbook published; included in Arctic Wolf MDR scope.

Retirement — when the underlying threat or asset no longer applies (e.g., legacy ETL decom).

All transitions are recorded as PRs against mct-detection-content and reviewed by a second D&R engineer per the four-eyes requirement.

7. Incident-Driven Use Cases

The Pebble Phish incident (March 14, 2024 — see Backgrounder §9) drove the creation of IA-02 (MFA fatigue / push bombing), IA-05 (auth following spray), and PE-04 (Okta admin role assignment outside change window). These remain Production-state and are reviewed annually as part of the post-incident control evaluation.
