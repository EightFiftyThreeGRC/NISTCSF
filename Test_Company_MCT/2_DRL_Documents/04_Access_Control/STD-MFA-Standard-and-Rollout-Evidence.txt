Multi-Factor Authentication Standard and Rollout Evidence

Document ID: STD-MFA-01 Version: 3.1 Effective date: January 1, 2026 Last reviewed: December 11, 2025 Next review: December 2026 Approver: Sarah Yoon, CISO Owner: Ben Olafsson, IAM Specialist (delegated by CISO) Distribution: All employees, contractors, and authorized third parties; published on internal wiki and Hyperproof; rollout evidence section refreshed quarterly

1. Purpose

This Standard specifies the technical requirements for multi-factor authentication ("MFA") at Meridian Care Technologies, Inc. ("MCT"), implements the authentication intent of POL-001 §3.6 and POL-IAM-01 §4, and provides the rollout evidence package supporting MCT's stated KPI: 100% workforce MFA coverage; 100% phishing-resistant MFA on privileged populations achieved Q4 2025.

Sections 1–8 establish the standing standard. Section 9 is the rolling rollout evidence package, refreshed quarterly to support SOC 2 Type II testing, HITRUST CSF r2 evidence, and HIPAA Security Rule compliance under 45 CFR §164.312(d).

2. Scope

This Standard applies to:

All MCT workforce authentication events through Okta.

All MCT cloud workforce access via AWS IAM Identity Center.

All customer-administrative authentication through the MeridianCare customer admin console.

All patient-facing authentication through Auth0 patient CIAM (subject to per-tenant configuration).

Break-glass authentication paths covered as documented exceptions per PROC-PAM-01.

3. Workforce MFA Standard

3.1 Universal requirement

MFA is mandatory for 100% of human workforce accounts (employees, contractors, interns) at every authentication event to MCT-managed systems. The default workforce MFA factor is Okta Verify with push and number-matching enabled (number-matching enforced as of October 2024 to mitigate MFA fatigue).

3.2 Permitted factors

The following factors are permitted for workforce authentication, in priority order:

FIDO2 / WebAuthn — required for in-scope populations per §4.

Okta Verify push (with number-matching).

Okta Verify TOTP (offline / backup).

3.3 Prohibited factors

SMS / voice OTP (prohibited as primary or secondary; not configured in Okta).

Knowledge-based "security questions."

Email OTP for routine authentication.

3.4 Recovery factors

Account recovery is mediated through (a) IT Operations via ticket with two-factor identity verification, or (b) self-service recovery using a paired FIDO2 backup key for in-scope populations. The IAM Specialist verifies a sample of recovery events monthly.

4. Phishing-Resistant MFA (FIDO2 / WebAuthn)

4.1 Mandatory populations

FIDO2 / WebAuthn is mandatory for the following populations. Full enforcement was achieved Q4 2025 (KPI: 100%; see §9).

All Information Security personnel (n=6).

All engineers and SREs with production access (n≈50 in Q4 2025; population varies with engineering hiring).

All IAM administrators and IT administrators (n=5).

All members of the Office of the CEO (n=6).

Any role with an entitlement to read or modify PHI in production (n≈70 inclusive of overlaps with the above; reconciled monthly against INV-Data-Classification-Inventory).

4.2 Approved authenticators

Each in-scope user is required to enroll at least two FIDO2 authenticators (one roaming + one platform, or two roaming) so that single-key loss does not lock the user out.

4.3 Enforcement

Okta authentication policy enforces FIDO2-only for in-scope populations. Non-FIDO2 factors are blocked at policy for these users. Policy logic (simplified):

IF user is member of Okta group "fido2-required"
  AND application is in catalog "in-scope-applications"
THEN allowed factor = WebAuthn ONLY

The fido2-required group is sourced from (a) Workday job-role attributes mapped to in-scope roles, (b) overlay groups for cross-functional roles (e.g., on-call rotation members), and (c) explicit additions for any role granted PHI production access via PROC-PAM-01 elevations.

4.4 PAM separation

Privileged identities (<user>.adm) are subject to the same FIDO2 requirement and require FIDO2 authentication within the prior 5 minutes of a JIT elevation request (per PROC-PAM-01 §5).

5. Customer-Administrative MFA Standard

MFA is mandatory for all customer-administrative accounts. Two paths:

Federated customers (37 of 47 customers as of Q1 2026): contractually required to enforce MFA at the customer IDP. MCT validates the SAML/OIDC assertion includes an MFA claim (amr value of mfa, pwd+otp, pwd+mfa, or phr).

Okta-hosted customer admins (10 of 47 customers): MCT-managed Okta authentication policy enforces Okta Verify minimum + FIDO2 recommended; FIDO2 will become mandatory for customer admins in Q3 2026 per the FY26 IAM roadmap.

6. Patient-Portal MFA Standard

Patient-portal MFA is configured per customer tenant in Auth0. The MCT default tenant template recommends:

MFA on first login from a new device (mandatory).

Risk-based step-up MFA (Auth0 attack-protection signals: impossible travel, suspicious IP, breached credentials).

TOTP and email OTP supported; SMS deprecated (still permitted in legacy tenants pending uplift).

Known coverage gap: A subset of customer tenants do not enforce step-up MFA on every patient login by tenant configuration choice. This is documented as risk register entry R8 ("Patient-portal MFA inconsistent across customer tenants"). The FY26 IAM roadmap includes a tenant-by-tenant uplift program targeting full step-up MFA by Q3 2026 for all customer tenants where the customer has not opted out in writing.

7. Exception Handling

7.1 Exception scenarios

Common exceptions:

New-hire pre-FIDO2-enrollment window (first 24 hours): Okta Verify acceptable; FIDO2 enrollment required as condition of in-scope access.

Hardware loss: emergency Okta Verify acceptable for 24 hours; new FIDO2 issued within 24 hours; security incident treatment if loss circumstances are unclear.

Travel without FIDO2: must request a temporary device-bound platform authenticator pre-trip.

Vendor / break-glass: covered in PROC-PAM-01 §6.

7.2 Exception process

All exceptions are requested via ServiceNow GRC, evaluated by the IAM Specialist, and approved by the CISO for any exception >24 hours. Exceptions >7 days require Security Steering Committee endorsement. All exceptions are logged in REG-EX-01.

8. Logging, Monitoring, and Reporting

8.1 Logging

All authentication events (success and failure) are logged in the Okta system log and aggregated to Datadog Cloud SIEM. Retention is 90 days hot in Datadog and 7 years cold in S3 per HIPAA §164.316(b)(2).

8.2 Monitored signals

MFA-fatigue patterns (≥3 push prompts within 5 minutes — alerted post-Pebble Phish).

Failed MFA attempts ≥5 within 10 minutes.

Authentication from unusual geography (Datadog GeoIP enrichment + Okta ThreatInsight).

FIDO2 fallback attempts on fido2-required users (should be zero by policy; any non-zero = P2 alert).

8.3 KPI reporting

The IAM Specialist publishes a monthly KPI dashboard to the Security Steering Committee:

Workforce MFA enrollment %.

FIDO2 enrollment % for in-scope populations.

Mean time to FIDO2 enrollment for new in-scope hires.

Exception count and aging.
